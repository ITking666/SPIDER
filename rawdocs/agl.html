ArDean Leith

Computer Scientist

M.S.   Computer Science, Rensselaer Polytechnic Inst.
Ph.D. Biology (Biophysics), University of Rochester

Employed by: Div. of Computational Biology and Molecular Imaging, at Wadsworth Labs

Ardean (that's an ancient Scottish name) has been involved in advancing computational techniques in biological imaging since 1985. He initially set up a data base for serial-section imagery and developed computer graphics techniques for recovery of depth information from stereoscopic electron microscopy. He also developed techniques and software for visualizing and interacting with images from electron microscopes. More recently he has worked on techniques for speeding and parallelizing the computations involved in single particle reconstructions from electron micrographs. This work appears within the SPIDER image processing software. He writes and maintains a lot of Web based documentation. He also manages more than 90 computers used in this biological imaging research.

In a previous existence Ardean worked on Cellular Biology in a tropical Marine Biology Laboratory, taught in three Universities, and was greatly influenced by experience in the U.S Peace Corps.

  • Develops and maintains software written in: C, C++, FORTRAN, Java, Perl, as well as several archaic languages.
  • Familar with: OpenGL, Inventor, X-Windows, HTML, Volume Rendering, NAG Explorer, Amira,....

Member of: Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, Association for Computing Machinery; ACS; and the American Association for the Advancement of Science

Ardean is fluent in English and Malay (Malaysian/Indonesian) and understands a bit of Mandarin
