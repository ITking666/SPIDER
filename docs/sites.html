WWW Sites for Biological Electron Microscopy

A list of some WWW sites that I am aware of. Lack of inclusion here implies nothing.

  • General WWW Sites for Biological Electron Microscopy

    • Sites for Image Processing Software for Visualization
      The NIH's Clinical Center has a good list of sites for software which they use.

    • Scientific Visualization Sites from the Numerical Aerospace Simulation Facility at NASA Ames Research Center.
      Contains a list of Visualization links (May not be current).

    • Web Links for Microscopes and Microscopy
      Good list of WWW sites for electron, light, confocal, laser, scanning probe, nuclear magnetic, and x-ray microscopy.

    • WWW Virtual Library: Microscopy
      Good listing of WWW sites for all aspects of light microscopy, electron microscopy and other forms of microscopy. (Last updated 2005?.)

    • Microscopy Vendors Database
      Good listing of microscopy vendor sites compiled by Kaker.Com.

    • Biomedical Imaging on the WWW
      Good listing of Biomedical EM sites compiled by P. V. Osta.

    • 3-Dimensional Electron Microscopy
      Home of the 3D-EM mailing list at National Center for Microscopy and Imaging Research.

    • The Boulder Laboratory for3-Dimensional Fine Structure
      A National Resource Center devoted to "the development of new technologies for specimen preparation and image analysis to study the three-dimensional structure of cells and tissues". Also home for: IMOD software.

    • National Center for Microscopy and Imaging Research (NCMIR)
      A research facility for "advancing three-dimensional microscopy and imaging of biological materials". Also home for: SYNU software.

    • The National Center for Macromolecular Imaging
      at Baylor College of Medicine. Also home for: EMAN software.

    • Microscopy & Microanalysis WWW Site (Argonne Nat. Lab)
      Has info on the Microscopy and Microanalysis programs at Argonne National Labs as well as a good list of links to other Microscopy sites.

    • Univ. of Minnesota Supercomputing Institute
      Good listing of Sites for Visualization Software

  • Serial Section & Stereo Reconstruction WWW Sites

    • Serial Section & Stereo Reconstruction Software
      My listing of sources that provide software.

  • Single Particle Reconstruction WWW Sites

    • Electron microscopy for Dummies
      (Hanspeter Niederstrasser, U. C. Berkeley, CA)
      Instructions for single particle reconstruction.

    • Electron Microscopy Software (Tim Baker, Purdue Univ)
      Some single particle reconstruction software.

    • Calidris (Manhemsvägen, Sweden)
      Some EM related commercial software and hardware.

    • Xmipp: An Image Processing Package for Electron Microscopy (Centro Nacional de Biotec., Univ. Autónoma de Madrid, Spain)
      Single particle reconstruction software.

    • ImageScience (Berlin, Germany)
      Some commercial single-particle averaging software including Imagic.

    • ScienceGL (ScienceGL, Quincy,MA)
      Some EM related visualization software.

    • Stanford University School of Medicine High Resolution Electron Microscope Facility for Biomedical Sciences (McMahan and Kornberg Labs, Stanford, CA)
      A Neurobiology tomography lab and a single particle reconstruction lab.

    • (Helen Saibil, Birkbeck College, London)

    • Eva Nogales' Lab, Lawrence Berkley Lab, CA
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Single particle analysis of Phosphorylase Kinase (Venien-Bryan, Lab. of Molecular Biophysics, Oxford Univ, UK)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Electron microscopy and image reconstruction of biological macromolecules (Bottcher Group, EMBL, Germany)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Automated Molecular Imaging Group (Scripps Research Inst., CA)
      SUPRIM, emScope, Leginon, & Viewit EM software.

    • Nikolaus Grigorieff's Lab (Brandeis Univ. RI)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • David DeRosier's Lab (Brandeis Univ. RI)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Shahid Khan's Lab ( Molecular Biology Consortium, Chicago, IL)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Pawel Penczek's Lab (Univ. of Texas-Houston Medical School, TX)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Holger Stark's Lab (Max-Planck-Institut, Germany)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • The Structural Electron Microscopy Facility at BUSM (Boston Univ. Medical College, Boston, MA)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Laboratory of Molecular Biology (MRC, Cambridge, UK)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • P. A. Bullough's Lab (Sheffield Univ, UK)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Phoebe L. Stewart's Lab (Vanderbilt Univ.)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Macromolecular Structure Group, David Agard and John Sedat Labs (University of San Francisco, CA)
      Provides software for single particle reconstruction.

    • Ken Downing's Lab (Lawrence Berkeley Lab, Berkeley, CA)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

    • Edward H. Egelman's Lab (Univ. Virginia, Charlottesville, VA)
      A single particle reconstruction lab.

  • Electron Tomography WWW Sites

    • National Center for Microscopy and Imaging Research (San Diego, CA)
      Provides software for electron tomography and an electron tomography resource.

    • Macromolecular Structure Group, David Agard and John Sedat (University of San Francisco, CA)

    • Image Science
      Image Science offers em2em software to convert images to/from common formats used in 3D electron microscopy"

    • Tietz Video and Image Processing Systems GmbH (Gauting, Germany)

    • FEI Company (Hillsboro, OR)

      Information on electron tomography techniques.

    • Electron microscopy for Dummies
      (Hanspeter Niederstrasser, U. C. Berkeley, CA)
      Practical info.

      SUPRIM, emScope, Leginon, & Viewit EM software.
